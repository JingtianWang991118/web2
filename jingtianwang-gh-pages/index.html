Research

    • 3D Structure Mediated Growth of Zero Dimensional Cs4SnX6 Nanocrystals
      Kaimin Xu, Qi Wei, Bing Yao, Wenjia Zhou, Rong Gao, Hao Chen, Hansheng Li, Jingtian Wang, Zhijun Ning
      Nanoscale
      In this paper, we developed a 3D structure mediated method to synthesize inorganic 0D tin perovskite NCs. The PLQY of Cs4SnBr6 NCs achieved 52%, which is the highest value for tin perovskite NCs up to now.
    • Reduction of Defect Density in Sn-based Perovsite Solar Cells by a Component Split Synthetic Method
      In this project, we suggested that a component split synthetic method which divided Formamidine Hydroiodide (FAI) into Formamidine Acetate (FAAc) and Ammonium Iodide (NH4I) can elevate the energy barrier of perovskite formation and slow down the formation of perovskite film, thus reducing defect density. So far, a power conversion efficiency of 14% has been achieved.
    • Formation of Low-Dimensional Perovskite Structure by Incorporating 2,2-Bipyridine Hydroiodide
      In this project, a large ammonium molecule, 2,2-Bipyridine (Bpy), was found to be able to guide the formation of low-dimensional layered perovskite structure.
    • ZnO Supported Ag@Pd Core@Shell Nanocatalysts in CO2 Hydrogenation to CH3OH
      In this project, we found that Ag@Pd/ZnO(EG) catalyst perform a higher CO2 conversion ad methanol selectivity in CO2 hydrogenation reaction than their counterparts supported on zinc oxide with different morphologies. SEM results indicate the microstructure of ZnO(EG) is nanosphere, which is different from ZnO(Me) and ZnO(H2O) nanorod. XRD results show the significant difference in crystal plane of these three kinds of zinc oxide. Combining these characterizations, we hypothesize defects on ZnO influence the reaction performance. Catalyst performance under higher activation temperature of 250℃ presents an transition of active sites between 240 and 250℃, which is a critical point for further analysis.
